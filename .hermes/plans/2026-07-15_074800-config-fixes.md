**Step 1: Add the keybind**

fsel 3.1.0 has `--dmenu` mode: piped lines become selectable entries, selection printed to stdout. Bind `SUPER+ESC` to a one-liner that pipes the three choices, reads the pick, and acts:

```nix
        # Power menu via fsel dmenu mode
        "$mod ESC, exec, sh -c 'echo -e \"Shutdown\nReboot\nSuspend\" | fsel --dmenu --no-exec | xargs -I{} sh -c \"case {} in Shutdown) systemctl poweroff;; Reboot) systemctl reboot;; Suspend) systemctl suspend;; esac\"'"
```

(If `--no-exec` isn't the right flag to just print the selection, use `--dmenu` alone and capture stdout — confirm fsel's dmenu output behavior during build/test. Alternative: `fsel --dmenu` reads stdin, prints chosen line to stdout; wrap with `$(...)` command substitution.)
